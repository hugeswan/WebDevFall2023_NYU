Ways of Seeing(Episode 1)

"But I can demonstrate the silence."

The most striking aspect of paintings themselves is their inherent silence and stillness. It's challenging to convey their stillness through a digital screen, as the lines on your screen are in constant motion, and even the pages of a book, while physically still, are not truly silent. However, the silence of paintings can be readily demonstrated. Occasionally, this uninterrupted silence and the profound stillness of a painting can be incredibly impactful. This experience seems to transcend conventional teachings about art. It's as though the painting, in its absolute stillness and soundlessness, transforms into a corridor that connects the moment it represents with the moment you are observing it. Something seems to travel down that corridor at a speed surpassing that of light, raising questions about our conventional way of measuring time. Paintings' unique qualities of being silent and still, coupled with their detachment from their original context, make them susceptible to manipulation. They can be used to advance arguments or convey messages that may be vastly different from their original intended meaning. Given their inherently silent and still nature, the most apparent way to manipulate paintings is by introducing movement and sound. When a painting is shown on film or television, its meaning can undergo even more radical transformations. These observations highlight the remarkable flexibility of paintings as a medium. Their silent and still characteristics, combined with their ability to be detached from their original context, open the door to an array of interpretations. The interpretation of a painting can vary widely based on who is observing it and how it is being interpreted. The images within a painting can be likened to words waiting to form a dialogue. The power lies in the hands of the interpreter, making the act of viewing a painting a deeply personal and subjective experience. It's a testament to the enduring, mutable, and profound nature of art that can transcend time, culture, and context.
